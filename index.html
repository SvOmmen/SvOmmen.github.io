Sandrien van Ommen

Postdoc at CNRS, LPP, Paris Descartes

sandrien.van-ommen[at]parisdescartes.fr


I'm an experimental linguist working on language processing and development. My psycholinguistic, developmental, phonetic and computational work are centered on phonology. How do listeners use the abstract sound structure of their language to process native and non-native speech? How do infants acquire the sound structure of their language and how do they use it to get a handle on other linguistic structure? Currently, I am especially fascinated by the versatility of prosody facilitating processing, distinguishing meaning and expressing pragmatic intent all at the same time. Phonologically, I am intrigued by the interplay of universal and language-specific characteristics of this music of language.


I am an experimental linguist working at Laboratoire Psychologie de la Perception, with Ranka Bijeljac-Babic and Thierry Nazzi, in collaboration with Universität Potsdam with Barbara Höhle and Natalie Boll-Avetisyan. In 'Multilevel Prosodic Processing in a Cross-Linguistic Perspective' (ANR-DFG), we focus on the language-specific prosodic development on the lexical and phrasal level in French- and German-learning infants. My PhD-work on the language-specific use of word stress in segmentation, with René Kager, took place in the context of 'Parsing and Metrical Structure: where Phonology meets Processing' (NWO) at Utrecht University and I did my work on mutual intelligibility (with Petra Hendriks, Dicky Gilbers, Charlotte Gooskens and Vincent van Heuven), linguistic distance (with John Nerbonne, Martijn Wieling and Charlotte Gooskens) and emphatic lengthening (With Jack Hoeksema and Dicky Gilbers) at the University of Groningen
